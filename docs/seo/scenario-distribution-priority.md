# Scenario Distribution Priority

This document defines which canonical `/scenario/[slug]` pages should get the strongest internal exposure and the earliest external distribution effort.

## Selection logic

Attack-page selection follows four rules:

1. Commercial value: payment, pricing, and scope-boundary pages outrank softer communication pages.
2. Anxiety strength: pages tied to money, margin, deposit risk, or active scope pressure outrank softer follow-up intent.
3. Intent distinctness: pages with clean search intent boundaries are safer to promote than pages that still overlap with nearby variants.
4. Cluster node value: pages that can anchor a cluster and pull users into next-step pages are better promotion targets than leaf pages.

## Promotion layers

### Layer 1: Primary attack pages

These pages should get the strongest internal exposure and be the first candidates for link building, partnership mentions, and scenario-level promotion.

#### Payment

- `ask-for-payment-politely`
- `final-payment-reminder`
- `start-before-payment`
- `deposit-not-paid-yet`
- `payment-extension-request`

#### Pricing

- `quote-too-high`
- `discount-request`
- `found-someone-cheaper`
- `lower-rate-after-proposal`
- `meet-their-budget`

#### Scope

- `extra-work-for-free`
- `unlimited-revisions`
- `changing-requirements-response`
- `out-of-scope-professionally`
- `scope-creep-polite-response`

#### Ghosting

- No current ghosting page is in the primary attack layer.
- Ghosting pages still matter, but they work better as cluster support and path continuation than as the first pages to push externally.

### Layer 2: Secondary support pages

These pages reinforce cluster coverage, capture adjacent intent, and route traffic toward the attack pages.

#### Payment

- `unpaid-invoice-follow-up`
- `payment-overdue-reminder`
- `second-payment-reminder`
- `overdue-invoice-no-response`
- `pay-later-request`

#### Pricing

- `same-scope-lower-price`
- `out-of-budget-but-interested`
- `best-price-before-signing`

#### Scope

- `extra-work-outside-scope`
- `adding-small-requests`
- `extra-page-request`
- `extra-revision-rounds`

#### Ghosting

- `client-no-response-follow-up`
- `after-client-ghosted`
- `no-response-after-proposal`
- `reviewing-internally-no-response`
- `contract-sent-no-response`
- `silent-after-discovery-call`
- `no-response-after-rate`

### Layer 3: Monitoring / low-priority pages

These pages stay live, stay indexable, and keep cluster breadth, but they should not absorb current distribution effort until data proves they deserve it.

#### Pricing and discovery probes

- `fixed-price-unclear-scope`
- `price-before-scope`
- `help-define-scope`
- `best-price-request`

#### Softer client-management pages

- `what-is-included`
- `unclear-process`
- `deliverables-vs-outcomes`
- `difficult-client-response`
- `rude-client-tone-response`
- `passive-aggressive-client-response`

## On-site exposure rule

- Primary pages should sort first in scenario-hub popular surfaces and cluster lists.
- Cluster-core pages should outrank other primary pages when the score is otherwise tied.
- Secondary pages should mainly appear as similar/next-step routes that feed users into stronger pages.
- Monitor pages stay available but should not displace primary pages in internal promotion surfaces.

## Distribution use

Use Layer 1 first for:

- outreach and backlinks
- guest mentions and partner placements
- manual internal-link priority reviews
- any limited promotion budget

Use Layer 2 to:

- reinforce cluster completeness
- support next-step journeys
- catch adjacent search intent without splitting focus

Use Layer 3 to:

- collect ranking and impression data
- identify future merge, promotion, or rewrite candidates
